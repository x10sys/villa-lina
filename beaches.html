Lovran

Beach Kvarner

North of Lovran about 900 meters away from the city center there is the Kvarner Beach which has a "Blue Flag“. In the bathing bay overgrown with vegetation there is a pebbled beach where you can find many cemented lounging places. These stretches fall terraced-like into the water. The interspersing trees provide shade over the cemented areas. Left of the beach cafés and of the sanitary facilities there is a stretch of pebbled beach under the trees.

At the center of the beach there is a playground and a viewing tower for the lifeguards, as well as changing rooms. The beach is easily accessible on foot through the Lungomare or by driving down the main street. There are parking spots along the street.

Lovran

Beach Peharovo

Peharovo Beach is a beautiful beach situated on the outskirts of the tourist resort known for its Mediterranean climate - Lovran. The beach is pebbly, but it is sandy at the entrance to the sea which attracts a large number of guests, especially families with small children who can play there for hours. There are natural floors where you can rent deck-chairs with or without beach umbrellas, as well as changing rooms.

At the entrance to the beach there is a restaurant that perfectly fits into this landscape and offers a wide range of drinks and ice cream. The beach Peharovo gathers the younger generation in the evening, who enjoy in the music until the early morning hours.

If you want to relax in the shade during the hot summer days, look for the place under the large pine tree tops in the background of the beach. The beach was awarded a Blue Flag as a symbol of purity and quality of the offer and the sea.

Ičići

Beach Ičići

The largest beach in Ičići is so central and extensive, that you can reach it on foot. The pebbled beach is around 500 meters long and in some areas has partially cemented sunning areas dispersed in between the sand. This is the reason why you have such comfortable access to the water and a flat, gently sloping shore.

The beach also has deserved its “Blue Flag”, keeping itself clean by providing facilities such as toilets and changing rooms.

This beach is popular with the young and the old and provides many opportunities for good food and leisure activities, such as water-skiing, jet-skiing and beach volleyball.

Opatija

Beach Slatina

The beach is surrounded by buildings and pines, has lounging areas, there are possibilities for renting sunshades and sun loungers, and there are also stairs with which they are easier to enter the sea. One narrow, artificial sandy beach with soft bottom and shallow coastline is very popular among small children.

Cafés and restaurants provide the pleasure of enjoying food and drink for adults, while children and younger will prefer to have fun on the playground or on jet skiing. Sanitary facilities, showers and changing cabins take care that nothing is missing on the beach. Due to the safety of guests there are also rescuers.

Mošćenička Draga

Beach Sipar

The 2 kilometer long pebbled Sipar Beach extends from the harbor close to the city center and is interrupted in some areas by the cliffs. The white cliffs descend into to the shallow shores and the turquoise sea waters, which children enjoy in particular.

From the beach, you have a stunning view of the Kvarner Bay and the picturesque stone-houses sitting on the edge.

Beach bars, flat waves, and diverse offers make the Sipar beach especially popular with a wide array of vacationers. Here there are beach bars, ice cream parlors, restaurants as well as a playground and possibilities for paddle boat and canoe rentals.

Boats can anchor in the water and the swimming area is separate. Sanitary facilities and changing rooms complement the rest of the facilities.

Brseč

Beach Klančac

One of the prettiest natural beaches. Not far away from Bršeč, a small medieval Istrian town located on a high cliff offering a beautiful view of the Kvarner Bay and islands, are some of the most beautiful beaches in Istria, among which the beach Klančac is definitely one that needs to be mentioned. Hidden in a small picturesque bay and hidden by cliffs, this pebble beach is considered among the most popular on the Adriatic.

Even though arriving to the beach must be on foot, thanks to its exceptional beauty the beach Klančac attracts a large number of swimmers who enjoy the peace and quiet and want to spend the day at the sea at a safe distance from the characteristic tourist crowds.
